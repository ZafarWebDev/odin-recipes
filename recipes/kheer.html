Kheer (Rice Pudding)

Description

Kheer, a beloved dessert in Pakistan, is a traditional rice pudding that holds a special place in the hearts of many. This creamy and rich dish is made by slowly cooking rice in full-fat milk until it becomes tender and absorbs the milk's flavors. The pudding is sweetened with sugar and often flavored with cardamom, which adds a warm, aromatic touch.

In Pakistani households, kheer is often garnished with a variety of toppings, including slivers of almonds, pistachios, and raisins, which not only enhance its visual appeal but also add texture and flavor. Some variations may include saffron for a luxurious touch and a beautiful golden hue.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups milk
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • ½ cup basmati rice
  • ¼ cup raisins
  • ½ teaspoon ground cardamom
  • ½ teaspoon rose water (Optional)
  • ¼ cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • ¼ cup chopped pistachio nuts

Steps

  1. Bring coconut milk, milk, and sugar to a boil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add rice, reduce the heat to low, and simmer until mixture thickens and rice is tender, about 20 minutes.
  2. Stir in raisins, cardamom, and rose water; cook for a few more minutes. Ladle into serving bowls and garnish with almonds and pistachios.
